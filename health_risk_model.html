üè• Problem Statement

Healthcare systems face a critical challenge: optimal patient-hospital matching. When a patient needs hospital care, how do we determine:

  • What is the patient's actual disease risk profile?
  • Which hospital has the right resources and specialization?
  • How do we allocate limited hospital resources fairly and efficiently?
  • Can we reduce patient wait times through intelligent referrals?

Traditional approaches rely on manual assessment and historical data. This project uses predictive machine learning to make data-driven decisions, improving outcomes and resource efficiency.

üèóÔ∏è System Architecture: Two-Layer ML Design

Layer 1: Disease Classification (Multi-Class Prediction)

Purpose: Identify the patient's primary disease predisposition

What it does: Analyzes patient health metrics (blood pressure, glucose levels, age, family history, lifestyle factors) and classifies them into one of three disease categories:

  • Hypertension Risk ‚Äî Cardiovascular disease predisposition
  • Stroke Risk ‚Äî Cerebrovascular event probability
  • Diabetes Risk ‚Äî Metabolic disorder classification

ML Approach: Multi-class classification model (trained on balanced dataset using SMOTE oversampling)

Output: Predicted disease category + confidence scores

Layer 2: Disease-Specific Risk Analysis

Purpose: Calculate precise risk scores for the identified disease

What it does: Once primary disease is identified, specialized models calculate a unique risk score (0-100) for each condition:

  • Hypertension Model: Analyzes blood pressure patterns, age, family history ‚Üí produces hypertension severity score
  • Stroke Model: Evaluates cerebrovascular risk factors (BP, cholesterol, smoking, atrial fibrillation) ‚Üí stroke probability score
  • Diabetes Model: Assesses metabolic markers (glucose, BMI, insulin resistance) ‚Üí diabetes risk score

ML Approach: Disease-specific regression/classification models with comprehensive feature engineering

Output: Quantified risk score for each disease (enables comparison and severity ranking)

Layer 3: Simulation & Hospital Referral (In Development)

Purpose: Use risk scores to optimize patient-hospital allocation

What it will do:

  • Simulate hospital capacities and specialization levels
  • Match patients to hospitals based on disease risk and facility capability
  • Optimize resource allocation to minimize wait times and improve outcomes
  • Evaluate allocation strategy efficiency through simulation

Current Status: Architecture designed, implementation in progress

üîß Technical Implementation

Data Engineering & Preprocessing

Challenge: Imbalanced dataset (many healthy patients, fewer with disease)

Solution: Applied SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) to balance class distribution, preventing model bias toward majority class

Feature Engineering

Developed comprehensive feature set covering:

  • Demographic Features: Age, gender, family history
  • Clinical Markers: Blood pressure, glucose, cholesterol, BMI
  • Behavioral Factors: Smoking, alcohol use, exercise frequency
  • Engineered Features: Risk ratios, interaction terms, temporal patterns

Model Selection & Training

Experimented with multiple algorithms (logistic regression, random forest, gradient boosting) and selected optimal models based on:

  • Classification accuracy on validation set
  • Precision/recall balance (healthcare context requires low false negatives)
  • Interpretability (doctors need to understand model decisions)
  • Inference speed (real-time predictions needed)

Technology Stack

Python scikit-learn pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn imbalanced-learn (SMOTE)
